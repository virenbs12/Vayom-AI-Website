B2B Function 3: Transaction deduplication (B2B)
Duplicate invoices, duplicate credits, and repeated payments are direct cash leakage. This workflow detects duplicates across AR activity and stops cash from leaving twice.
•	It detects potential duplicates across invoices, credit memos, payments, and order entries, even when references do not match perfectly.
•	It groups duplicates into a single review bundle with supporting record links for quick confirmation.
•	It reduces write-offs and cleanup work by catching issues before they cascade into disputes.
Visual instruction:
Use a “Duplicate cluster” visual: one cluster card that expands to show all related transactions (Invoice A, Invoice B, Credit Memo, Payment), with a “Mark as duplicate” decision button.

B2B Function 4: Transaction validation (B2B)
Validation prevents incorrect billing before it becomes customer friction. This workflow checks invoice lines against contracted price lists, discount rules, and effective dates.
•	It validates line-level price, discount, and fee logic against the active agreement for that customer.
•	It highlights exceptions with the specific contract reference and the exact transaction fields that failed validation.
•	It outputs an “Approve / Flag / Resolve” queue that finance and RevOps can work through quickly.
Visual instruction:
Use a validation queue table: “Account | Invoice | Rule violated | Evidence | Owner | Status,” with a “View rule” flyout and a “View evidence” flyout.

B2B Function 5: Rebates, incentives, and deductions control
Rebate claims and deductions can quietly drain cash when eligibility and documentation are inconsistent. This workflow ties claims to contract terms and supporting records.
•	It identifies claims that lack support, exceed eligibility, or do not align to the contracted program terms.
•	It highlights where deductions patterns are increasing for specific accounts or product lines.
•	It gives a clean package for recovery conversations: what is valid, what is not, and why.
Visual instruction:
Use a rebate claim packet mock: left side “Program terms summary,” right side “Claim lines,” and a bottom “Missing documentation” checklist.

B2B Function 6: Payment terms and cash collection leakage
Late payments, missed early-pay discounts, and inconsistent enforcement create cash flow drag. This workflow surfaces patterns early and ties them back to accounts and terms.
•	It identifies early-pay discounts taken incorrectly and late-pay behavior that is trending worse.
•	It highlights accounts with frequent short-pays or recurring deductions.
•	It outputs a prioritized account list for AR follow-up with supporting evidence.
Visual instruction:
Use a timeline card per account showing “Invoice date → Due date → Payment date” with a simple “On-time / Late” badge and a link to supporting transactions.

B2B Function 7: Channel partner and distributor compliance
Channel leakage happens when partner terms, pricing, and territory rules are not consistently followed. This workflow monitors adherence and exposes exceptions.
•	It flags transactions that violate channel pricing, territory, or partner agreement terms.
•	It aggregates exceptions by partner, region, and product family to spot systematic drift.
•	It supports partner conversations with evidence that is easy to review and share.
Visual instruction:
Use a partner scorecard tile: “Compliance rate,” “Top violations,” and “At-risk partners,” each linking to exception lists.

B2B Function 8: Revenue readiness and audit trail packaging
Close gets painful when teams cannot show the “why” behind numbers. This workflow keeps revenue-relevant decisions traceable and review-ready.
•	It captures a clear trail of what changed, who reviewed it, and which records support the decision.
•	It helps teams resolve mismatches earlier instead of discovering them during close.
•	It provides audit-ready summaries without forcing teams into spreadsheet back-and-forth.
Visual instruction:
Use an “Evidence timeline” component: a chronological list of actions (Detected → Reviewed → Corrected → Approved) with links to source records and excerpts.
