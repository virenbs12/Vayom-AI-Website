B2C Function 3: Returns and refund accuracy
Returns are where margin disappears if reasons, policies, and outcomes are inconsistent. This workflow highlights repeat refunds, out-of-policy approvals, and patterns that increase return-to-origin events.
•	It summarizes return reasons and outcomes with clear evidence for each case.
•	It highlights outliers by product, warehouse, carrier route, and customer segment so operations can intervene.
•	It flags cases that should be investigated for repeat behavior, process gaps, or policy misalignment.
Visual instruction:
Use a returns “reason-to-cost” dashboard tile: a simple bar list (Reason → Total refund amount → Return rate) with a click-through to a case list.

B2C Function 4: Shipping cost and fulfillment leakage
Shipping overcharges and fulfillment mismatches quietly drain cash. This workflow compares expected vs actual shipping costs and catches fulfillment errors that create avoidable reships and credits.
•	It finds shipments where carrier charges exceed expected rates based on zone, weight, and service level.
•	It highlights fulfillment exceptions (split shipments, wrong service level, late delivery patterns) tied to refund or credit impact.
•	It outputs an action list for logistics teams: dispute, renegotiate, or tighten shipping rules.
Visual instruction:
Use a carrier invoice reconciliation visual: “Expected vs Actual” with a variance column, plus filters for Carrier, Service Level, and Zone.

B2C Function 5: Subscription billing hygiene
Subscription businesses leak cash through failed renewals, duplicate retries, misapplied credits, and mismatched plan terms. This workflow highlights billing noise that reduces retained revenue.
•	It identifies renewals that failed, repeated charge attempts, and subscriptions that received duplicate credits.
•	It groups issues by plan, cohort, and payment method so teams can fix the biggest drivers first.
•	It creates a prioritized “recover now” list and a separate “prevent next cycle” list.
Visual instruction:
Use a subscription “health grid”: cards for Failed renewals, Duplicate retries, Credit issues, each opening a drill-down list with evidence links.

B2C Function 6: Customer service impact mapping
Support tickets, chats, and complaints are early indicators of future refunds and churn. This workflow connects service signals to measurable revenue impact so teams act earlier.
•	It links common complaint themes to downstream outcomes like refunds, chargebacks, and cancellations.
•	It highlights accounts and products with rising service friction before costs spike.
•	It produces a weekly “top drivers” summary with drill-down to the supporting interactions.
Visual instruction:
Use a “Service-to-impact” Sankey-style mock (no complex charting needed): Complaint theme → Refunds / Returns / Cancellations, with clickable counts.
